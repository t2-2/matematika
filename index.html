Tense 1 of 6

Present Simple

When to use it: habits, routines, facts, general truths, schedules.

✔ Affirmative Subject + base verb (+s/es for he/she/it)
✖ Negative Subject + don't / doesn't + base verb
? Question Do / Does + Subject + base verb?
⚠️ He / She / It: always add -s or -es to the verb.
He works · She teaches · It goes · Use doesn't in negative, never don't.
I eat breakfast every day.
אני אוכל ארוחת בוקר כל יום.
routine
She doesn't like coffee.
היא לא אוהבת קפה.
negative — she/he/it → doesn't
Do they live in Tel Aviv?
האם הם גרים בתל אביב?
question

Signal Words

always · usually · often · sometimes · never · every day / week / year · on Mondays

Tense 2 of 6

Present Continuous

When to use it: actions happening right now, temporary situations, future plans.

✔ Affirmative Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing
✖ Negative Subject + am/is/are not + verb+ing
? Question Am/Is/Are + Subject + verb+ing?
Subjectam/is/areExample
Iam (I'm)I am sleeping.
He / She / Itis ('s)She is reading.
You / We / Theyare ('re)They are playing.
✏️ Spelling rules for -ing:
• run → running (double consonant after short vowel)
• make → making (drop silent -e)
• play → playing (vowel + y → just add -ing)
✔ Present Continuous
She is studying now.
I am leaving tomorrow.
NOT with these verbs
know · love · want · need · believe · have (possession)
"Stative" verbs — never use -ing!

Tense 3 of 6

Past Simple

When to use it: completed actions in the past at a specific time.

✔ Regular Subject + verb+ed
✔ Irregular Subject + past form (must memorize!)
✖ Negative Subject + didn't + base verb
? Question Did + Subject + base verb?
💡 In negatives and questions, always use the base verb — did does the past work!
✔ "Did she go?"   ✖ NOT "Did she went?"

Tense 4 of 6

Past Continuous

When to use it: an action that was in progress at a specific moment in the past, often interrupted by another action.

✔ Affirmative Subject + was/were + verb+ing
✖ Negative Subject + wasn't/weren't + verb+ing
⚡ Key pattern: Past Continuous + when + Past Simple
The long action (Cont.) was happening when the short action (Simple) interrupted it.
I was studying when she called.
הייתי לומד כשהיא התקשרה.
long action (was studying) interrupted by short action (called)
While they were eating, the power went out.
בזמן שהם אכלו, החשמל הלך.
while + continuous / when + simple
SubjectVerb to Be
I / He / She / Itwas (+ing)
You / We / Theywere (+ing)
Tense 5 of 6

Future:
will & going to

🔮 will — spontaneous / predictions▾
✔ AffirmativeSubject + will + base verb
✖ NegativeSubject + won't + base verb
I will help you. (spontaneous decision)
It will rain tomorrow. (prediction)

Signal: probably · maybe · I think · I'm sure

📅 going to — plans / evidence▾
✔ AffirmativeSubject + am/is/are going to + base verb
✖ NegativeSubject + am/is/are not going to + base verb
She is going to study medicine. (plan)
Look at those clouds — it is going to rain. (evidence)

Signal: tonight · next week · soon · I've decided

Tense 6 of 6

Present Perfect

Connects the past to the present — a past action with a present result or relevance.

✔ Affirmative Subject + have/has + past participle
✖ Negative Subject + haven't/hasn't + past participle
? Question Have/Has + Subject + past participle?
🔑 he/she/it → has  ·  I/you/we/they → have
I have visited Paris. (life experience — when? not important)
ביקרתי בפריז.
She has lost her keys. (past action, present result — still lost!)
היא איבדה את המפתחות שלה.
I have never eaten sushi. (never = zero times in life)
מעולם לא אכלתי סושי.

Signal Words

already · yet · ever · never · just · since · for · recently · so far

✔ Present Perfect
I have seen that film.
(no specific time)
✔ Past Simple
I saw that film yesterday.
(specific time → use past simple)

Modal Verbs

Modals add meaning (ability, permission, obligation, possibility) to the main verb. Always followed by base verb.

can
ability / permission
"I can swim." / "Can I leave?"
could
past ability / polite request
"She could run fast." / "Could you help?"
must
strong obligation / certainty
"You must wear a seatbelt." / "He must be tired."
mustn't
prohibition
"You mustn't smoke here."
should
advice / recommendation
"You should eat more vegetables."
shouldn't
negative advice
"You shouldn't stay up late."
may
possibility / formal permission
"It may rain." / "May I sit here?"
might
weaker possibility
"She might come to the party."
📌 No -s for he/she/it! ✔ "She can drive." ✖ NOT "She cans drive."
No "to" after modal (except have to): ✔ "You must go." ✖ NOT "must to go."

Grammar

Conditionals
Types 0, 1 & 2

⚙️ Zero Conditional — general truth / scientific fact▾
StructureIf + Present Simple, Present Simple
If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
אם מחממים מים ל‑100°, הם רותחים.
If I eat too much, I feel sick.
1️⃣ First Conditional — real / likely future▾
StructureIf + Present Simple, will + base verb
If it rains, we will stay home.
אם ירד גשם, נישאר בבית.
If you study hard, you will pass the exam.
💡The if-clause uses Present Simple even though we're talking about the future!
2️⃣ Second Conditional — unreal / hypothetical▾
StructureIf + Past Simple, would + base verb
If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world.
אם היה לי מיליון דולר, הייתי מטייל בעולם.
⚠️Use were for all subjects: "If I were you..." (NOT "was")

Passive Voice

Used when the action is more important than the person doing it, or when we don't know who did it.

FormulaSubject + be (correct tense) + past participle + (by + agent)
TenseActivePassive
Present SimpleThey make cars here.Cars are made here.
Past SimpleShe wrote the letter.The letter was written by her.
Present PerfectThey have built a bridge.A bridge has been built.
will (Future)He will fix the car.The car will be fixed.
ModalYou must do this.This must be done.
🔄 How to transform Active → Passive:
1. Object of active → Subject of passive
2. Verb → correct tense of "be" + past participle
3. Subject of active → "by + agent" (optional, often omitted)
Active: Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
Passive: Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
